About

Hi! I'm Amalie and I am a research fellow in astrophysics. I am part of the CartographY group at University of Birmingham.

My main scientific focus is Galactic archaeology -- a subfield of astrophysics which aims at unravling the physics of galaxies by studying the galaxy we live within, the Milky Way. Our challenge is that the Universe evolves on time scales far longer than human life times, making it difficult to study when what happened. As stars have been around cosmically for a long time, we can treat stars as fossil records and study how they have been shaped and influenced over billions of years. The characteristics of stars leave important clues about how the Milky Way has came to be the galaxy we see today, so learning to read the stars allow us to understand our galactic past.

I am generally very interested in astrostatistics and stellar inference, so how we can learn about the stars in the galaxy from the light we observe. My research focuses on utilizing stellar inference in various contexts, particularly on stellar populations. I compute the orbits of stars and correlate it with their chemical abundance patterns as well as their ages. The precise stellar ages I normally work with are determined through the study of stellar pulsations known as asteroseismology. The integration of these observations enables us to conduct detailed analyses of the Milky Way.

Precise and accurate stellar inference is key to my work. I am part of the core developement team of BAyesian STellar Algorithm (BASTA), which is a flexible and statistically robust pipeline for the inference of stellar properties, such as the masses, radii, and ages of stars.

When I am not studying stars, I enjoy running, playing chess, crafting, watching musicals, and puzzling with coding projects.
